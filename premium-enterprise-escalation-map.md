# Premium Enterprise Escalation Map

## Purpose
This document defines how premium enterprise questions should be escalated when the answer cannot be given directly from current source materials.

## Escalation Principles
- route fast rather than speculate
- use the narrowest qualified owner
- preserve current product truth
- do not convert exploratory interest into commitments

## Escalation Map

| Question Area | Primary Escalation Owner | Supporting Owners | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sovereign, residency, or regulated hosting | Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead | Sovereign Deployment Architect, Privacy Lead, Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | These topics change legal, technical, and privacy posture |
| Security diligence beyond published docs | Customer Assurance / Security Questionnaire Lead | Security Lead, Engineering Lead | Keeps answers consistent and evidence-based |
| Privacy commitments beyond current policy | Privacy Lead | Legal Counsel, Security Lead | Prevents undocumented privacy commitments |
| Pricing exceptions or premium packaging | CRO / Revenue Lead | Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead, CFO / Finance Lead | Prevents informal commercial promises |
| Contract redlines and terms | Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | Legal Counsel, CRO / Revenue Lead | Prevents unsupported legal acceptance |
| Accessibility commitments beyond published position | Accessibility Lead | QA / Reliability Lead, Product Lead | Prevents unsupported conformance statements |
| Public trust or buyer-facing claim wording | Trust Center / Public Documentation Owner | Repository Steward / Documentation Systems Lead, Marketing / Communications Lead | Keeps public-facing materials coherent and truthful |

## AI Rule
When uncertain, the AI should say that the question requires formal review and identify the correct owner rather than improvise an answer.

## Summary
Fast, disciplined escalation is part of premium enterprise behavior. It is better to route a question cleanly than to answer it loosely.
