# Privacy Policy

## Purpose
This document defines the current default privacy position for Monyawn at the platform level.

It should be treated as an operational policy draft and must be reviewed by qualified legal and privacy counsel before publication or contractual use.

## Privacy Principles
- collect only what is needed to operate the workflow
- explain data handling in clear language
- give users appropriate visibility into their data
- support deletion, correction, and retention controls where applicable
- avoid surprise secondary uses of user data

## Categories Of Data
Monyawn may process:
- account and profile information
- uploaded resumes and job descriptions
- notes, messages, interview materials, and related workflow artifacts
- system-generated outputs
- workflow and audit events
- support and onboarding communications
- optional sensitive support profiles only when the user explicitly enables them in the local workspace

## Why Data Is Processed
Monyawn processes data to:
- provide guided workflow functionality
- generate and review opportunity-related outputs
- save and resume user progress
- support reporting, support, security, and quality operations
- meet contractual or legal obligations where applicable

## Customer And User Control
Users or customer organizations should be able to understand:
- what data they provide
- what data the system generates
- what data may be visible to admins in organization-sponsored environments
- how data can be corrected, exported, or deleted where supported

## Data Ownership
Unless otherwise stated in contract, customer-provided content should be treated as customer or user data, not as Monyawn-owned content.

## Retention
Retention periods may vary by:
- product tier
- contract terms
- account type
- legal or operational requirements

Retention rules should be documented in implementation or account-specific materials where they differ from the default.

## Subprocessors And Third Parties
Where Monyawn relies on third-party service providers, those providers should be tracked and disclosed in a controlled subprocessor list where applicable.

## Cross-Border Data Handling
If Monyawn operates across regions, cross-border handling rules must be reviewed by the Privacy Lead and Legal Counsel before new commitments are made.

## Security Relationship
Privacy commitments depend in part on the security controls described in the security overview and related internal controls.

## AI And Data Use
Any AI usage involving customer or user data must align with:
- this privacy policy
- the AI governance policy
- applicable contract terms

If data is used for model improvement, evaluation, or training beyond baseline service delivery, that use must be clearly documented before it is represented as active.

## User Requests
Monyawn should maintain an internal process for:
- data access requests
- deletion requests
- correction requests
- privacy complaints or inquiries

## Governance
Primary owners:
- Privacy Lead
- Legal Counsel

Consulted owners:
- Security Lead
- Product Lead
- Customer Success Lead

## Summary
Monyawn should handle user and customer data in a way that is clear, limited, reviewable, and contract-aware. If the company cannot clearly explain a data practice, it should not assume that practice is approved.

## Local-Only Sensitive Support Rule
When sensitive support paths are enabled for issues such as layoffs, firing, re-entry, or background concerns:
- the feature must remain opt-in
- the data should remain local-only by default
- the data should not be exported unless the user explicitly includes it
- the platform should avoid forcing disclosure during standard onboarding
