# Premium Commercial Packaging Rules

## Purpose
This document defines how Monyawn should package premium enterprise opportunities without making unsupported product, legal, or sovereignty commitments.

## Packaging Principles
- package around current product truth
- do not use pricing creativity to hide delivery or compliance gaps
- keep support, deployment, and diligence posture aligned to current documentation
- route non-standard asks before treating them as valid offer components

## Current Product Packaging Truth
The current product posture is:
- local-first
- export-first
- multi-opportunity by default
- AI-assisted with human review expectations
- not a documented Monyawn-hosted sovereign offering

Premium commercial packaging must preserve those boundaries.

## Allowed Packaging Dimensions
Premium conversations may vary by:
- account type
- implementation support level
- enablement and onboarding intensity
- documentation and buyer-assurance support
- support responsiveness
- use-case complexity
- customer-managed deployment assumptions where explicitly reviewed

## Controlled Packaging Dimensions
These may not be treated as default commercial options without review:
- sovereign hosting
- regional residency guarantees
- non-standard security commitments
- non-standard privacy commitments
- custom legal terms
- premium support promises beyond documented support posture
- future-state product features framed as committed deliverables

## Premium Offer Structure
A premium enterprise package may include:
- implementation planning
- buyer diligence coordination
- documentation and trust-center support
- enablement and training
- premium review cadence
- controlled support and escalation routing

It must not imply:
- undocumented infrastructure commitments
- undocumented compliance posture
- undocumented contract acceptance
- undocumented uptime or hosting commitments

## Packaging Review Triggers
Commercial packaging must be reviewed when a deal includes:
- government or regulated-market expectations
- residency or sovereignty expectations
- custom redlines
- non-standard support or SLA terms
- non-standard price exceptions
- multi-region or high-risk deployment assumptions

## Ownership
Primary owners:
- CRO / Revenue Lead
- Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead

Supporting owners:
- CFO / Finance Lead
- Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead
- Customer Assurance / Security Questionnaire Lead
- Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead

## Summary
Premium packaging is not just price shaping. It is disciplined packaging of what the company can responsibly support today, with escalation when the buyer asks for more than that.
