# CEO Premium Sovereign Enterprise Expectations Matrix

## Purpose
This document compares CEO-level premium sovereign enterprise expectations against current Monyawn repo and platform reality.

It is intentionally conservative. Scores should reflect current truth, not aspiration.

## Rating Scale
- `1-3` = materially below enterprise expectation
- `4-5` = partial or emerging capability
- `6-7` = credible early enterprise posture
- `8-9` = strong premium enterprise posture
- `10` = exceptional, repeatable, evidence-backed posture

## Recommended Execution Sequence

| Phase | Focus | Primary Owners | Expected Lift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Premium narrative and trust coherence | Repository Steward / Documentation Systems Lead, Mission / Purpose Lead, Brand Strategy Lead | README, trust-center, and executive narrative move from strong to premium |
| Phase 2 | Sovereign and diligence posture | Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead, Customer Assurance / Security Questionnaire Lead, Security Lead, Privacy Lead | The company can answer sovereign and premium diligence questions without improvising |
| Phase 3 | Commercial and contracting discipline | CRO / Revenue Lead, Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead, Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | Premium enterprise conversations become more structured and less founder-dependent |
| Phase 4 | Deployment and environment credibility | Sovereign Deployment Architect, Engineering Lead, Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead | Product posture becomes clearer for local-only vs enterprise-hosted vs sovereign discussions |
| Phase 5 | Executive scoring and maintenance | Benchmarking And Evaluation Lead, CEO, President / GM | CEO expectations become a repeatable operating review instead of a one-time exercise |
