# MSA Redline Guidance

## Purpose
This document gives Monyawn an internal guide for handling master services agreement redlines and related enterprise contracting pressure.

It is intended to help legal, CRO, deal desk, and executives move consistently without implying commitments that exceed current product truth.

This is an internal operating guide, not legal advice.

## Redline Principles
- contract language must stay aligned to current product posture
- legal flexibility must not create undocumented product, security, privacy, or deployment commitments
- redlines that change data custody, hosting posture, sovereignty, support, or compliance meaning must be routed
- it is better to narrow or decline a term than to accept a promise the product does not currently support

## Current Product Truth That Must Be Preserved
- the current product is local-first
- Monyawn does not currently rely on company-side retention of user opportunity data for standard use
- Monyawn is not currently a documented sovereign-hosted platform
- Monyawn should not imply certifications, compliance, or residency guarantees without explicit evidence

## Redline Categories

| Category | Default Position | Primary Owner | Must Be Consulted |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard commercial cleanup | Reviewable | Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | CRO / Revenue Lead |
| Pricing or payment changes | Reviewable with approval | CFO / Finance Lead | CRO / Revenue Lead, Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead |
| Support or SLA expansion | Escalate | Customer Success Lead | Operations Lead, CRO / Revenue Lead |
| Security commitments beyond published posture | Escalate | Security Lead | Customer Assurance / Security Questionnaire Lead, Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead |
| Privacy, retention, or data-use changes | Escalate | Privacy Lead | Legal Counsel, Security Lead |
| Hosting, residency, or sovereignty terms | Escalate | Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead | Sovereign Deployment Architect, Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead, Privacy Lead |
| Product roadmap or feature commitments | Escalate | Product Lead / AI Product Architect | Engineering Lead, CRO / Revenue Lead |
| Public-sector or regulated-market clauses | Escalate | Government Contracting Lead | Legal Counsel, Security Lead, Accessibility Lead |
| Terms that contradict current product truth | Decline or rewrite | Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | CEO when material |

## Acceptable Redline Patterns
- clarifying language that does not change actual product posture
- reasonable contract cleanup that preserves current trust and technical boundaries
- buyer language that accurately reflects local-first and export-first behavior

## Escalate Patterns
- hosted-service assumptions
- regional data residency commitments
- sovereign or isolated deployment commitments
- custom security warranties or compliance language not already documented
- expanded support or uptime promises
- audit rights or reporting obligations beyond current operating readiness
- roadmap commitments tied to commercial terms

## Decline Patterns
- clauses that require Monyawn to accept unsupported sovereign hosting obligations
- clauses that assume company-side custody of user opportunity data in contradiction to the current product
- clauses that require undocumented certifications or compliance status
- clauses that force product behavior the current implementation does not support

## Suggested Response Style
- acknowledge the request calmly
- state that Monyawn uses controlled review for non-standard terms
- preserve current product truth
- offer revised language where possible instead of improvising a yes

## Internal Escalation Path
1. Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead
2. CRO / Revenue Lead and Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead
3. Controlled-domain owner based on topic
4. CEO or President / GM for material exceptions
