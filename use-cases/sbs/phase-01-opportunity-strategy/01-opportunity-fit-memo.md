# Opportunity Fit Memo — SBS Software Engineer (Design Tools)

## Role Snapshot
SBS builds advanced design and data integration software for utilities, telecoms, and critical infrastructure. The role focuses on a next-generation electrical substation design platform using C#/.NET, desktop engineering tools, Autodesk integrations, and reliability/performance in geometry-heavy workflows.

## Direct Fit Areas
### Strong Fit
- C# and .NET
- desktop engineering tooling
- Autodesk ecosystem experience
- AutoCAD / Inventor adjacency
- engineering software mindset
- debugging platform issues
- data integrity and governed workflows
- collaboration with technical and domain stakeholders

### Partial Fit
- geometry-heavy and model-driven systems
- performance optimization
- CI/CD and engineering standards
- testability and maintainability

### Gap Areas
- explicit Revit API evidence
- explicit electrical substation / utility domain experience
- explicit automated test depth in the resume

## What Makes You Attractive for This Role
1. You already operate in the class of systems where correctness, platform constraints, and engineering workflow fidelity matter.
2. You have real Autodesk development credibility, which reduces ramp risk versus a generic .NET developer.
3. You have experience stabilizing toolchains, integrations, and deployment patterns, which matters in design software ecosystems.
4. You can likely add value beyond coding alone: workflow clarity, architecture hygiene, and tooling discipline.

## What Makes This Opportunity Risky
1. It may be leveled lower than your experience.
2. It could prefer a more narrowly specialized Revit developer.
3. Your resume may read broader and more senior than what a mid-level requisition expects.

## Panel Recommendation
**Pursue selectively.**  
Proceed if:
- the team values Autodesk breadth and engineering-tooling rigor, not just direct Revit years
- compensation lands near the top of the posted band or better
- there is clear evidence of growth toward deeper technical ownership

Withdraw or de-prioritize if:
- they insist on substantial production Revit API history as a hard gate
- title/level materially undercuts your market path
- the interview signal suggests they want a narrower implementation profile than your trajectory

## Positioning Thesis
"I am a strong fit for Autodesk-centric engineering software where correctness, performance, and workflow fidelity matter. My deepest evidence today is in Inventor, AutoCAD, Vault, .NET desktop tooling, and enterprise-grade engineering integrations. I can contribute immediately in Autodesk-heavy design systems while ramping quickly on Revit- or domain-specific edges."
