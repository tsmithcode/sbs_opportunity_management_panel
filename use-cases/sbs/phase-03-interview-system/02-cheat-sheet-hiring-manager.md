# Interview Cheat Sheet — Hiring Manager

## What the Hiring Manager Cares About
- Can you actually build features?
- Can you debug hard issues?
- Can you work with domain experts?
- Will you raise code quality and engineering rigor without creating drag?
- Are you adaptable enough for Revit/domain ramp?

## 6 Proof Themes
1. Autodesk add-in modernization
2. Desktop engineering tools and workflow fidelity
3. Debugging and issue stabilization
4. Reliability patterns: validation, retry/backoff, idempotency, logging
5. Cross-functional translation between technical and domain stakeholders
6. Measurable results

## Your Best Stories
### Story 1 — Autodesk modernization
Show platform upgrade, compatibility, rollout control, and maintainability.

### Story 2 — Throughput and workflow quality
Show how structured inputs and automation improved drawing throughput and reduced loops.

### Story 3 — Governance without bureaucracy
Show that you improve quality systems while keeping teams moving.

## Questions to Ask
- What are the most technically difficult features on the roadmap this year?
- How much of the work is new feature delivery versus stabilization/refactoring?
- Where does Revit sit in the stack relative to other Autodesk or SBS systems?
- What distinguishes the strongest engineers on this team?

## What Not to Do
- Do not dominate the discussion with enterprise architecture.
- Do not make the role sound smaller than your experience.
- Do not talk around implementation details.
