# Interview Cheat Sheet — Recruiter / Initial Screen

## What They Are Screening For
- compensation fit
- level fit
- communication clarity
- whether your background is plausibly relevant despite Revit gap
- whether you sound motivated by the actual role

## Core Message
You are not a generic architect seeking any title. You are a hands-on Autodesk/.NET engineering software builder whose experience maps well to complex design tools.

## 5 Talking Points
1. Strong C#/.NET background in production systems.
2. Real Autodesk platform experience: Inventor, AutoCAD, Vault.
3. Desktop and workflow-driven engineering tooling.
4. Experience improving stability, rollout discipline, and system correctness.
5. Interest in design software where domain accuracy matters.

## If They Ask About Revit
Recommended answer:
"My most direct production Autodesk API evidence today is stronger in Inventor, AutoCAD, and Vault than in Revit specifically. What gives me confidence here is that the broader operating model is familiar: learning the domain workflow, understanding the API constraints, and translating that into reliable engineering software."

## If They Ask Why SBS
Recommended answer:
"SBS is interesting because it is engineering software, not generic application development. The focus on correctness, design fidelity, and Autodesk-based tooling is much closer to my strongest experience."

## Watchouts
- Do not sound overqualified and disengaged.
- Do not apologize for the architect title.
- Do not imply you are only using the role as a stopgap.
