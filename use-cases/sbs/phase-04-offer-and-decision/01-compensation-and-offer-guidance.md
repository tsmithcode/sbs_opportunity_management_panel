# Compensation and Offer Guidance

## Posted Package
- Base salary: **$140,000 - $160,000**
- Bonus: **up to 10% annual discretionary bonus**

## Interpretation
For a true mid-level design-tools engineer, this is a credible band. For someone whose resume reads senior/architect, the question is not just base pay but whether:
- the actual level is higher than the title suggests
- growth path is real
- work content is strategic enough to justify the move

## Negotiation Posture
### If early-stage
Do not negotiate aggressively before mutual interest is clear.

### If late-stage
Ask for:
- confirmation of level
- base target within or above top of band if justified
- bonus details
- review cycle timing
- promotion/growth expectations

## Decision Filters
Accept only if most are true:
- strong manager
- meaningful Autodesk/design-tools ownership
- credible growth path
- comp at or near top of range if no equity exists
- role strengthens your long-term market story

## Red Flags
- "mid-level" used to cap compensation despite senior contribution expectations
- vague advancement path
- heavy dependence on Revit depth they know you do not yet have
- weak technical leadership or unclear product direction
