# Enterprise Readiness Package

## Purpose
This document defines the minimum business, legal, security, accessibility, procurement, and operational materials Monyawn should be ready to provide when an enterprise, government buyer, educational institution, or other organization asks to do business.

It is written as a readiness checklist, not as legal advice. Final contract language, certifications, representations, and regulatory commitments should be reviewed by qualified counsel, security, compliance, and finance stakeholders before use.

## Company Positioning
Monyawn should be presented as:
- an AI-native opportunity and career workflow platform
- available as a local-first workflow product today, with future SaaS and AGaaS paths where appropriate
- designed for guided, accessibility-first, human-reviewable workflows
- suitable for enterprise, public-sector, education, and workforce use cases

## Required Readiness Materials

### 1. Corporate and procurement packet
Monyawn should maintain:
- legal company name and DBA information
- principal business address
- website and official contact channels
- tax forms such as W-9 or W-8
- invoicing and remittance instructions
- procurement contact and billing contact
- standard supplier onboarding responses

### 2. Government and public-sector packet
If selling to U.S. public-sector buyers, Monyawn should be ready with:
- UEI
- active SAM.gov registration
- CAGE code if applicable
- representations and certifications as required by the contracting vehicle
- ability to complete security and accessibility questionnaires
- records retention and public-sector data handling position

If selling outside the U.S., Monyawn should maintain the equivalent registration and procurement profile required by target jurisdictions.

### 3. Security packet
Monyawn should be ready to provide:
- security overview document
- architecture and data-flow summary
- authentication and authorization model
- encryption at rest and in transit summary
- logging and monitoring summary
- vulnerability management process
- incident response process
- secure SDLC summary
- backup and disaster recovery summary
- pen test summary or roadmap
- premium enterprise diligence routing path

### 4. Privacy and data governance packet
Monyawn should maintain:
- privacy policy
- data processing addendum
- subprocessors list
- data retention and deletion schedule
- data subject request process
- cross-border transfer position if applicable
- customer data ownership statement
- training data and model usage statement
- local-only data custody statement if user data is not retained on company systems
- sovereign and residency posture statement for current versus future-state claims

### 5. Accessibility packet
Because Monyawn is positioned as an accessibility-first workflow product, it should maintain:
- accessibility statement
- target conformance level, such as WCAG 2.2 AA
- VPAT or equivalent accessibility conformance report
- testing summary covering keyboard, screen reader, zoom, and mobile use
- accessibility support contact
- remediation SLA for high-severity accessibility defects

### 6. AI governance packet
Monyawn should clearly document:
- where AI is used in the product
- which actions require human review
- how evidence and confidence are shown
- how unsupported claims are prevented
- how users can correct, override, or reject outputs
- how model and prompt changes are governed
- whether customer data is used for training and under what conditions
- how candidate story, coaching, and compensation education are governed

### 7. Legal and contracting packet
Monyawn should be ready with:
- mutual NDA template
- master services agreement
- order form template
- DPA
- security addendum
- SLA
- acceptable use policy
- support policy
- pilot agreement template
- controlled redline and sovereignty-escalation guidance

### 8. Support and operations packet
Monyawn should define:
- support hours and channels
- severity levels
- escalation path
- target response times
- maintenance and outage communications
- incident communications process
- customer success or onboarding process
- export/import support expectations for a local-only product
- premium enterprise question escalation path

### 9. Premium sovereign and diligence packet
Monyawn should maintain:
- sovereign posture and residency guide
- premium enterprise diligence response matrix
- approved answer bank for common premium buyer questions
- escalation map for sovereign, legal, privacy, security, and pricing questions

## Buyer-Specific Requirements

### Enterprise buyers
Most enterprise buyers will expect:
- security questionnaire completion
- legal review packet
- privacy review packet
- SSO roadmap or support details
- uptime and support expectations

### Government buyers
Government buyers may additionally require:
- formal vendor registration
- accessibility documentation such as VPAT
- records retention clarity
- stronger data handling controls
- procurement vehicle alignment
- public-sector-specific contract clauses

### Education and nonprofit buyers
These buyers may prioritize:
- privacy and student data position
- accessibility assurance
- predictable pricing
- low-friction onboarding

## What We Should Be Able To Say Clearly
Monyawn should be able to answer these buyer questions directly:
- Who are you legally, and where are you based?
- What data do you collect, store, and process?
- Do you retain user opportunity data on your own systems, or does the user keep custody?
- Who owns customer data?
- Is customer data used to train models?
- What certifications do you have today, and what is on the roadmap?
- How do you handle incidents and breaches?
- What accessibility standard do you target?
- What support and uptime commitments do you offer?
- What AI safeguards and human-review gates are built into the product?

## Release Gate For Selling To Organizations
Monyawn should not actively pursue enterprise or government sales until the following are true:
- standard contract packet exists
- privacy and security responses are documented
- accessibility position is documented
- support and escalation path are documented
- buyer-facing trust materials exist
- internal owners are assigned for procurement, security, privacy, accessibility, and support

## Summary
If an organization asks, "What do you need from us to start, and what can you provide so we can approve you as a vendor?" Monyawn should be able to answer with a single organized readiness package rather than ad hoc responses.
