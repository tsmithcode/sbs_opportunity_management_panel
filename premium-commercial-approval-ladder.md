# Premium Commercial Approval Ladder

## Purpose
This document defines how premium enterprise pricing, packaging, contracting, and commitment requests should be approved.

## Approval Principles
- the higher the commitment, the higher the review
- delivery, legal, privacy, security, and sovereignty implications matter as much as price
- no one should approve a promise outside their authority

## Approval Ladder

| Request Type | Primary Approver | Must Be Consulted | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard packaging within documented policy | CRO / Revenue Lead | CFO / Finance Lead | Must remain within current product truth |
| Pricing exception or discount outside standard thresholds | CFO / Finance Lead | CRO / Revenue Lead, Deal Desk / Commercial Operations Lead | Financial risk and precedent must be reviewed |
| Non-standard support or premium response commitments | Customer Success Lead | Operations Lead, CRO / Revenue Lead | Must align to documented support capacity |
| Buyer diligence commitments beyond published docs | Customer Assurance / Security Questionnaire Lead | Security Lead, Privacy Lead, Compliance Program Manager | Do not turn diligence requests into commitments without review |
| Sovereign, residency, or environment-bound commitments | Data Residency / Sovereignty Lead | Sovereign Deployment Architect, Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead, Privacy Lead, Security Lead | Always controlled-domain review |
| Custom contract redlines | Enterprise Counsel / Contracting Lead | Legal Counsel, CRO / Revenue Lead, CFO / Finance Lead | Do not accept informally |
| Product roadmap commitments tied to a deal | Product Lead / AI Product Architect | Engineering Lead, CRO / Revenue Lead, CEO when material | Must not be implied through commercial pressure |
| High-risk or strategic deal exceptions | Founder / CEO | President / GM, CRO / Revenue Lead, CFO / Finance Lead, Legal Counsel | Executive exception path |

## AI Rule
The AI may explain that commercial, legal, or sovereign requests require approval. It must not approve those requests.

## Summary
Premium enterprise maturity is visible when approval boundaries are obvious and non-standard requests move through a disciplined ladder rather than founder improvisation.
