# Pricing And Approval Policy

## Purpose
This document defines the default internal pricing and commercial approval posture for Monyawn.

It is an internal operating policy draft, not a published price list and not a contractual commitment.

## Policy Principles
- pricing should be intentional, not improvised
- approvals should match deal risk and commitment level
- the AI must not make pricing promises outside approved policy
- discounts, special terms, and exceptions must be reviewable

## Pricing Scope
Pricing may vary by:
- account type
- deployment model
- product tier
- support level
- enterprise requirements
- AGaaS or managed-service scope

## Approval Rules
The following should require approved commercial review:
- non-standard pricing
- discounts outside approved thresholds
- custom terms affecting cost or support load
- government or regulated-market commitments
- large-volume or multi-region deals
- sovereign, residency, or controlled deployment expectations
- premium packaging that changes support, diligence, or legal posture

## Commercial Discipline Rules
- pricing must stay aligned to the current product and trust posture
- commercial packaging must not imply unsupported hosting, sovereignty, compliance, or support commitments
- deal-specific terms must follow the approval ladder rather than informal founder or sales judgment
- premium enterprise pursuit should be qualified before non-standard effort is invested

## AI Rules
AI must not:
- promise final pricing
- approve discounts
- approve custom commercial terms
- imply company acceptance of non-standard pricing structures

AI may:
- explain the general pricing model if documented
- state that pricing and terms depend on approved commercial review
- route the request to the correct company owner

## Governance
Primary owners:
- CFO / Finance Lead
- Sales Lead

Consulted owners:
- CEO
- Legal Counsel
- Customer Success Lead

## Summary
Commercial flexibility is allowed, but not through improvisation. If pricing or approval rules are not documented, the AI and staff should route the matter for review rather than guessing.
